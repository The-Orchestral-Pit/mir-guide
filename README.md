# MIR Guide — A community map for Music Information Retrieval

A community map of the people, labs, communities, and events shaping Music Information Retrieval.
Maintained as part of The Orchestral Pit, which runs a daily MIR research feed on Twitter/X ([@theOrchestralPit](https://x.com/theOrchestralPit)).

## What this is

MIR Guide maps the *who* and *where* of Music Information Retrieval — the labs doing the research, the communities where people gather, and the events where they meet. It is not a paper or library catalog; for those, see [yamathcy/awesome-music-informatics](https://github.com/yamathcy/awesome-music-informatics) and the [related lists](#related-lists) at the bottom of this page. The goal is a living reference for anyone trying to orient themselves in the MIR ecosystem.

## How to use this guide

Each entry carries a `source_type` — academic, industry, community, or media — that lets you filter by context. Browse the sections in order or jump directly to what you need. Filtering will become interactive on the companion site when it ships.

## Communities

- **[AUDITORY Mailing List](https://auditory.mcgill.ca/)** — Email list for auditory perception, psychoacoustics, and computational auditory scene analysis research. Managed by McGill University.
- **[ISMIR Mailing List](https://ismir.net/)** — Official mailing list of the International Society for Music Information Retrieval with approximately 1,800 members. Primary channel for CFP announcements, jobs, and community news.
- **[MIR Slack](https://ismir.net/)** — Slack workspace for the MIR research community, accessible via the ISMIR website with approximately 400 members. Used for informal discussion, questions, and announcements.
- **[r/musicir](https://www.reddit.com/r/musicir/)** — Reddit community for Music Information Retrieval discussions, paper links, and tools. Low traffic but useful for archived questions and entry-level discussions.
- **[The Sound of AI Slack](https://valeriovelardo.com/the-sound-of-ai-community/)** — Largest AI-audio Slack community with approximately 9,000 members, founded by Valerio Velardo. Covers deep learning for audio, music generation, and careers in AI audio engineering. *People: Valerio Velardo.*
- **[WIMIR](https://wimir.wordpress.com/)** — Widening Inclusion in Music Information Retrieval — an ISMIR initiative promoting diversity in the field through a Google Group, annual mentoring program, and workshop at each ISMIR conference.

## Events

### Conferences

- **[Music Tectonics Conference](https://www.musictectonics.com/)** — Annual music-technology conference in Santa Monica focused on the business and strategy of music innovation. Runs alongside the Music Tectonics podcast and Rock Paper Scanner newsletter. *People: Dmitri Vietze.* *(Santa Monica, CA, annual)*
- **[SXSW Music](https://www.sxsw.com/music/)** — Music track of South by Southwest, held each March in Austin. Includes a growing music-technology programming strand alongside the core showcase and conference programme. *(Austin, TX, annual)*

### Festivals

- **[Amsterdam Dance Event](https://www.amsterdam-dance-event.nl/)** — Annual electronic music conference and festival in Amsterdam covering the business, technology, and culture of dance music. Features a growing programme of AI and music-technology panels. *(Amsterdam, Netherlands, annual)*
- **[Ars Electronica](https://ars.electronica.art/)** — Annual festival and conference in Linz for art, technology, and society. Music, sound, and AI are recurring themes across exhibitions, talks, and the Prix Ars Electronica competition. *(Linz, Austria, annual)*
- **[Eurosonic Noorderslag](https://esns.nl/)** — Annual European music showcase conference in Groningen combining an industry conference with live performances. Increasingly features music-technology and AI programming tracks. *(Groningen, Netherlands, annual)*
- **[Sónar+D](https://sonarplusd.com/)** — Creative technology strand of the Sónar festival in Barcelona, featuring the recurring AI & Music powered by S+T+ARTS programme alongside talks, demos, and art-tech collaborations. *(Barcelona, Spain, annual)*

### Meetups

- **[Boston AI Music Meetup](https://www.meetup.com/boston-ai-music-meetup/)** — Monthly meetup for AI music researchers and practitioners in the Boston area, organised by Christian Steinmetz. Events are held at MIT Media Lab, Suno, and other Cambridge venues. *People: Christian Steinmetz.* *(Cambridge, MA, monthly)*
- **[London Audio & Music AI Meetup](https://www.meetup.com/london-audio-and-music-ai-meetup/)** — London-based meetup for audio and music AI practitioners from industry and academia. Talk archives are available on GitHub at lamai-meetup/meetups and via the group's YouTube channel. *(London, UK)*
- **[Munich Music Labs](https://lu.ma/munichmusiclabs)** — Student initiative at TU Munich bridging AI and music through monthly Knowledge Sharing Sessions, the annual HACKATUNE hackathon, and community events for students and practitioners. *(Munich, Germany, monthly)*
- **[Water & Music](https://lu.ma/water-and-music)** — Event series by Cherie Hu at the intersection of music, technology, and business. Covers streaming economics, generative AI, and music-tech strategy. *People: Cherie Hu.*

## Labs

### Europe

- **[AudioLabs Erlangen](https://www.audiolabs-erlangen.de/)** — Joint lab of FAU Erlangen-Nuremberg and Fraunhofer IIS, led by Meinard Müller. Home of the FMP notebooks and textbook on music processing fundamentals. *People: Meinard Müller.*
- **[C4DM — Centre for Digital Music](https://c4dm.eecs.qmul.ac.uk/)** — Interdisciplinary research centre at Queen Mary University of London covering music analysis, synthesis, and interaction. Home of the EPSRC AIM Centre for Doctoral Training. *People: Mark Sandler, Simon Dixon, György Fazekas.*
- **[COSMOS — Computational Shaping and Modeling of Musical Structures](https://cosmos.isd.kcl.ac.uk/)** — ERC-funded research initiative at King's College London investigating how musical structures affect cardiovascular physiology, with applications in music-based cardiac diagnosis and therapy.
- **[CP Lab — Institute of Computational Perception](https://www.jku.at/en/institute-of-computational-perception/)** — Research institute at JKU Linz led by Gerhard Widmer and Markus Schedl, working on music information retrieval, automatic music transcription, and music recommendation. *People: Gerhard Widmer, Markus Schedl.*
- **[DCML — Digital and Cognitive Musicology Lab](https://www.epfl.ch/labs/dcml/)** — Research lab at EPFL led by Martin Rohrmeier studying harmony, counterpoint, and musical form using corpus analysis, computational modelling, and cognitive approaches. *People: Martin Rohrmeier.*
- **[INESC TEC — Sound and Music Computing Lab](https://www.inesctec.pt/en/laboratories/laboratory-of-sound-and-music-computing)** — Sound and music computing research group at INESC TEC Porto, led by Matthew Davies, with focus on rhythm analysis, beat tracking, and music signal processing. *People: Matthew Davies.*
- **[IPEM — Institute for Psychoacoustics and Electronic Music](https://www.ugent.be/lw/kunstwetenschappen/ipem/en)** — Research institute at Ghent University studying embodied music cognition, music and motion, and expressive interaction through psychoacoustic and computational methods.
- **[IRCAM](https://www.ircam.fr/)** — Paris-based research institute and concert hall dedicated to acoustic music research, computer music, and sound-space interaction. Manages the ISMIR mailing list and hosts the Max/MSP ecosystem. *People: Geoffroy Peeters.*
- **[MTG — Music Technology Group](https://www.upf.edu/web/mtg)** — Research group at Universitat Pompeu Fabra Barcelona covering music analysis, synthesis, and retrieval. Creators of the Essentia library and the Freesound audio sharing platform. *People: Xavier Serra, Dmitry Bogdanov, Frederic Font.*
- **[OFAI — Intelligent Music Processing Group](https://www.ofai.at/)** — Music and machine learning research group at the Austrian Research Institute for Artificial Intelligence in Vienna, working on score-performance alignment and expressive music analysis.
- **[TU Wien — IFS Music Information Retrieval](https://www.ifs.tuwien.ac.at/)** — MIR research group within the Institute of Information Systems Engineering at TU Wien, led by Peter Knees, with focus on music search, recommendation, and interfaces for music creation. *People: Peter Knees.*
- **[Tampere Audio Research Group](https://research.tuni.fi/arg/)** — Audio research group at Tampere University led by Tuomas Virtanen, specialising in sound event detection, acoustic scene analysis, and audio source separation. *People: Tuomas Virtanen.*

### North America

- **[CCRMA — Center for Computer Research in Music and Acoustics](https://ccrma.stanford.edu/)** — Multidisciplinary research centre at Stanford University for music technology, acoustics, and computer music. Hosts the musicinformationretrieval.com course notebooks. *People: Julius O. Smith, Iran Roman.*
- **[CIRMMT — Centre for Interdisciplinary Research in Music Media and Technology](https://www.cirmmt.org/)** — Multi-university research centre hosted at McGill University's Schulich School of Music, covering music performance, recording, cognition, and computing. *People: Ichiro Fujinaga.*
- **[GTCMT — Georgia Tech Center for Music Technology](https://gtcmt.gatech.edu/)** — Interdisciplinary centre at Georgia Tech for music technology research and education, covering music computing, interactive systems, and music AI. *People: Alexander Lerch, Gil Weinberg.*
- **[Interactive Audio Lab](https://interactiveaudiolab.github.io/)** — Research group at Northwestern University led by Bryan Pardo, focused on audio source separation, music generation, and human-in-the-loop audio editing systems. *People: Bryan Pardo.*
- **[LabROSA — Laboratory for the Recognition and Organization of Speech and Audio](https://labrosa.ee.columbia.edu/)** — Columbia University research lab led by Dan Ellis working on speech and audio recognition, music analysis, and the AUDITORY mailing list. *People: Dan Ellis.*
- **[MARL — Music and Audio Research Laboratory](https://steinhardt.nyu.edu/marl)** — Research lab at NYU Steinhardt led by Juan Pablo Bello, focusing on music information retrieval, urban sound analysis, and open-source tools including Librosa and mir_eval. *People: Juan Pablo Bello, Brian McFee.*

### Asia-Pacific

- **[AMAAI Lab](https://amaai.dorienherremans.com/)** — Audio, Music, and AI lab at Singapore University of Technology and Design led by Dorien Herremans, working on generative models for music, affective computing, and multimodal AI. *People: Dorien Herremans.*
- **[MAC Lab — KAIST](https://mac.kaist.ac.kr/)** — Music and Audio Computing Lab at KAIST Graduate School of Culture Technology, led by Juhan Nam. Research spans music information retrieval, music AI, and open course materials. *People: Juhan Nam.*
- **[Media Interaction Group — AIST](https://staff.aist.go.jp/m.goto/index-e.html)** — Research group at Japan's National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology, led by Masataka Goto. Creators of the RWC Music Database and numerous MIR systems. *People: Masataka Goto.*
- **[Music and AI Lab — Academia Sinica](http://mac.citi.sinica.edu.tw/)** — Research lab at Academia Sinica Taiwan's Research Center for IT Innovation, led by Yi-Hsuan Yang. Formerly the Music and Audio Computing Lab, now focused on music AI and generative models. *People: Yi-Hsuan Yang.*
- **[SMC Lab — NUS](https://smcnus.org/)** — Sound and Music Computing Lab at the National University of Singapore, led by Ye Wang, with emphasis on MIR applications in music education and music-enhanced health. *People: Ye Wang.*

### Industry

- **[Deezer Research](https://research.deezer.com/)** — Research team at Deezer covering music recommendation, audio signal processing, and NLP. Creators of the Spleeter open-source source separation library.
- **[Google DeepMind — Magenta](https://magenta.tensorflow.org/)** — Google's research project exploring generative models for music and art, producing tools such as MusicVAE, NSynth, and Magenta Studio. Part of the broader Google DeepMind organisation. *People: Doug Eck, Adam Roberts.*
- **[Harmonai](https://www.harmonai.org/)** — Open-source generative audio research organisation backed by Stability AI, releasing models and tools for music generation including Dance Diffusion and Stable Audio. *People: Jordi Pons, Zach Evans.*
- **[Meta FAIR — Audiocraft](https://ai.meta.com/research/)** — Audio AI research team within Meta Fundamental AI Research responsible for MusicGen, AudioCraft, and EnCodec. Publishes models and code as open source. *People: Alexandre Défossez, Jade Copet.*
- **[Sony CSL Paris — Music Team](https://cslmusicteam.sony.fr/)** — Music AI research team at Sony Computer Science Laboratories Paris working on AI-assisted composition and production, including DrumGAN, Diff-A-Riff, and PESTO. *People: Stefan Lattner, Alain Riou.*
- **[Spotify Research](https://research.atspotify.com/)** — Spotify's research division covering music recommendation, audio understanding, NLP, and user behaviour modelling. Publishes papers, datasets, and open-source libraries. *People: Sebastian Ewert.*

## Learning

- **[Audio Content Analysis (audiocontentanalysis.org)](https://www.audiocontentanalysis.org/)** — Open textbook and dataset hub by Alexander Lerch covering audio content analysis, feature extraction, and music information retrieval with accompanying code and data. *People: Alexander Lerch.*
- **[Audio Signal Processing for Music Applications](https://www.coursera.org/learn/audio-signal-processing)** — Coursera course by Xavier Serra (UPF/MTG) and Juan Pablo Bello (NYU) covering audio signal processing fundamentals with applications to music analysis and synthesis. *People: Xavier Serra, Juan Pablo Bello.*
- **[FMP Notebooks — Fundamentals of Music Processing](https://www.audiolabs-erlangen.de/FMP)** — Interactive Jupyter notebooks by Meinard Müller accompanying the Fundamentals of Music Processing textbook. Covers audio features, structure analysis, rhythm, harmony, and source separation. *People: Meinard Müller.*
- **[intro2ddsp](https://intro2ddsp.github.io/)** — Online tutorial by Ben Hayes introducing differentiable digital signal processing, covering the principles and practical applications of gradient-based DSP in music and audio. *People: Ben Hayes.*
- **[ISMIR Tutorials Archive](https://ismir.net/resources/tutorials/)** — Archive of tutorials presented at the annual ISMIR conference, spanning MIR fundamentals to advanced topics in deep learning, evaluation, and multimodal music understanding.
- **[KAIST CTP431 — Fundamentals of Computer Music](https://mac.kaist.ac.kr/~juhan/ctp431/index.html)** — Open course materials by Juhan Nam (KAIST) covering music signal processing, MIDI, synthesis, and machine learning for music. A foundational resource for the GCT634 graduate course. *People: Juhan Nam.*
- **[musicinformationretrieval.com](https://musicinformationretrieval.com/)** — Interactive Jupyter notebooks by Iran Roman (CCRMA) covering core MIR topics including feature extraction, beat tracking, chord recognition, and source separation. *People: Iran Roman.*
- **[The Sound of AI YouTube](https://thesoundofai.com/)** — YouTube tutorial series by Valerio Velardo covering deep learning for audio, audio signal processing for machine learning, and careers in AI audio. Accessible entry point for beginners. *People: Valerio Velardo.*
